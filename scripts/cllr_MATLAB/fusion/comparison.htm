Logistic Regression Fusion vs Minimum-MSE fusion.

 

Below, we give APE-plots of linear logistic regression and minimum-MSE fusions of the scores of the 9 component systems that were entered by Spescom DataVoice in the 2005 NIST Speaker Recognition Evaluation.

 

(These are lazy experiments, in the sense that we measured performance on the same data that was used to train the fusion weights. We found that for these low-capacity fusions and calibrations, this (dubious) practice does not influence the results much. I.E. the results on new test data is much the same.)

 

The APE-plots show discrimination and calibration loss. We give plots for both kinds of fusion, and for three different prior-weightings (p in the plots). In all three cases we see:

·       Discrimination (green bar-graph) is very similar in both cases. (The DET-curves also coincide.)

·       Overall calibration (red bar-graph) is better for the logistic regression, but local (at logit p) calibration (vertical distance between red and green curves) seems to be better for the MSE case.

·       Although the prior-weighting does make a difference in both methods, logistic regression is much less sensitive to this parameter.
